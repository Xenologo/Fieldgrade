# SAP rival strategy

## Positioning rule

Do not position Fieldgrade as a full ERP replacement. Position it as the evidence-governance layer that sits beside transactions and proves what happened, why it happened, who reviewed it, and what evidence supports it.

## Headline

> **SAP runs the transaction layer; Fieldgrade proves the evidence layer.**

## Where to compete

Lead with the operational edges that remain weak even in SAP-heavy organisations:

- uncontrolled spreadsheets
- emailed PDFs
- screenshots and local folders
- supplier evidence
- QA notes and deviations
- investigation timelines
- review trails
- AI-assisted outputs with uncertain provenance
- audit preparation packs

## Where not to compete first

Do not lead with:

- finance
- payroll
- HR
- inventory planning
- purchasing workflows as a system of record

Those areas make Fieldgrade look under-scoped. Fieldgrade is stronger as the proof layer attached to messy evidence than as the primary transaction engine.

## Sales wedge

Use this message consistently:

> **Turn messy operational evidence into sealed, reviewable, replayable, exportable proof.**

## Product ladder

1. **Fieldgrade Core** proves provenance and evidence integrity.
2. **Fieldgrade ProofOps** packages the review workflow and audit pack.
3. **Fieldgrade GovAI** packages AI accountability.
4. **Fieldgrade FoodQA** packages audit-ready QA and production evidence.
5. **Fieldgrade Enterprise Bridge** connects exports and imports to ERP, QMS, and adjacent systems.

## Commercial path

Win with setup-led deployments and domain packs. Once teams rely on Fieldgrade for audits, investigations, and accountability reports, add bridge integrations rather than broad transactional features.
