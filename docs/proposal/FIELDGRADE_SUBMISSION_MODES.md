# Fieldgrade Submission Modes

This note separates Fieldgrade's strongest submission routes so the same repository can be framed honestly for different calls, pilots, and partner conversations. The matrix stays short; this document carries the fuller routing guidance.

## A. Frontier AI Benchmarking Datasets

**Lead positioning.** Present Fieldgrade as a FAIR, provenance-preserving governance layer for frontier-AI benchmark datasets and benchmark-supporting evidence bundles.

**Best artifact to show.** Show the synthetic demo dataset together with the export manifest and readiness validation output.

**What evidence is needed.**

- Source provenance and ingestion metadata for each object.
- Annotation and review history that shows who changed what and when.
- Admissibility tiers and claim status fields that separate raw inputs from reviewed benchmark evidence.
- A partner benchmark scenario showing how a benchmark owner would inspect or export the bundle.

**What not to claim.**

- Do not claim that Fieldgrade itself is the benchmark.
- Do not claim benchmark leadership, regulator endorsement, or production dataset operations.
- Do not imply that the synthetic demo is already a national-scale benchmark asset.

**Next partner requirement.** A benchmark owner, evaluation consortium, or research infrastructure partner that can supply a real benchmark-review workflow and approved sample records.

## B. Frontier AI Discovery

**Lead positioning.** Present Fieldgrade as the evidence and audit layer for AI-assisted discovery workflows that still require explicit human review.

**Best artifact to show.** Show a local workflow that moves from ingestion to annotation, review, and export, supported by the audit trail and admissibility states.

**What evidence is needed.**

- Review checkpoints showing that AI-assisted outputs are not treated as final decisions by default.
- Claim status and admissibility fields that separate observation, interpretation, and controlled extension.
- A reproducible local run path that demonstrates the audit-ready workflow without relying on a hosted service.
- Partner-specific examples of research traces, notes, or generated outputs that need review.

**What not to claim.**

- Do not claim fully autonomous science.
- Do not claim that Fieldgrade validates scientific truth or experimental outcomes.
- Do not imply that the current demo replaces lab systems or specialist reviewers.

**Next partner requirement.** A university lab, research institute, or discovery team willing to map a real review process onto the current evidence model.

## C. ProofOps commercial pilot

**Lead positioning.** Present Fieldgrade as a local-first ProofOps workspace that helps small teams convert messy operating evidence into reviewable proof packs.

**Best artifact to show.** Show a completed proof-pack style export and the local demo path that produces it.

**What evidence is needed.**

- A clear local install and run path.
- A small but complete review workflow that ends in an exportable evidence pack.
- Operator-visible provenance, review decisions, and explanation fields.
- A pilot scenario with real document classes or proof obligations from a target SME.

**What not to claim.**

- Do not claim that Fieldgrade is a full ERP, QMS, or compliance platform.
- Do not claim automatic certification or auditor sign-off.
- Do not imply that the current demo is already a production rollout.

**Next partner requirement.** An SME design partner with a real proof workflow such as QA evidence, delivery evidence, assurance packets, or customer response packs.

## D. FoodQA pilot

**Lead positioning.** Present Fieldgrade as an evidence-governed review layer for food quality, release, and exception-handling records.

**Best artifact to show.** Show a FoodQA-style release packet with source records, annotations, reviewer states, and an export manifest.

**What evidence is needed.**

- Batch or lot evidence with provenance and ingestion timestamps.
- Human review states for deviations, approvals, and superseded records.
- A partner-specific export pack that mirrors how quality evidence is assembled for review.
- Clear data-handling boundaries for any commercially sensitive records.

**What not to claim.**

- Do not claim regulator approval, HACCP automation, or legal compliance by default.
- Do not present Fieldgrade as replacing the partner's quality system.
- Do not imply that the demo already contains live operational food records.

**Next partner requirement.** A food producer, processor, packer, or QA consultancy that can provide a controlled pilot scenario and non-sensitive sample evidence.

## E. GovAI governance pilot

**Lead positioning.** Present Fieldgrade as a human-reviewable evidence register for accountable AI use, review decisions, and exportable governance records.

**Best artifact to show.** Show the governance dashboard or advisory path together with the audit trail and explicit review-state model.

**What evidence is needed.**

- A documented separation between AI assistance, review, and approval.
- Evidence of local-first handling for sensitive records or policy materials.
- Risk controls, review notes, and decision trails that a governance lead can inspect.
- A partner scenario describing which AI-assisted activities need to be recorded.

**What not to claim.**

- Do not claim automated policy compliance or legal determination.
- Do not claim that Fieldgrade replaces governance committees, auditors, or responsible-AI officers.
- Do not imply that the advisory layer auto-approves decisions.

**Next partner requirement.** A public-sector team, responsible-AI lead, or governance function willing to test a bounded accountability workflow.

## F. Advanced-materials provenance extension

**Lead positioning.** Present Fieldgrade as a controlled extension of the evidence model for experiment batches, microscopy, sensor traces, QA records, and reviewer decisions.

**Best artifact to show.** Show a materials-oriented provenance packet and the schema-extension path rather than claiming a full domain product.

**What evidence is needed.**

- Sample experiment or batch records with provenance notes and review states.
- A clear mapping for microscopy, sensor, or QA artifacts into the existing evidence model.
- Domain review criteria from a materials partner.
- A validation plan showing what would need to be proven in a real lab setting.

**What not to claim.**

- Do not claim scientific breakthrough, materials validation, or deployment in a live lab.
- Do not present this route as already domain-complete.
- Do not imply that the current synthetic demo proves materials outcomes.

**Next partner requirement.** A materials lab, RTO, or demonstrator partner that can validate the schema extension against real but shareable experimental workflows.
