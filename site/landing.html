How Fieldgrade turns work into evidence

Make the transformation obvious: from scattered project artefacts to reviewed, sealed, exportable proof.

Capture

Bring in notes, screenshots, diagrams, datasets, spreadsheets, logs, code references, and AI outputs.

Witness

Name what the material is, where it came from, who owns it, and why it matters to a claim.

Seal

Lock identity with fingerprints, timestamps, manifests, and provenance events that can be replayed later.

Review

Keep authority explicit with confidence, admissibility, risk, and reviewer sign-off states.

Export

Produce evidence packs with matrices, registers, manifests, PDFs, and ZIP bundles.

Reuse

Keep searchable project memory without turning the product into a generic file cabinet.

Bring in the messy evidence

Start with notes, screenshots, diagrams, dataset extracts, code references, and a draft claim list.

Witness what each item is

Show object ID, source type, provenance note, uploader, timestamp, SHA-256 fingerprint, and review state.

Link claims without hiding uncertainty

Attach claim status, confidence, admissibility, milestone context, and missing-evidence notes.

Seal the evidence object

Display the bundle ID, canonical hash, manifest, included files, and provenance chain.

Make review explicit

Move the record through Raw, Ingested, Annotated, Reviewed, Exported, or Superseded with reviewer notes.

Export the dossier

Deliver a proposal pack with a claim matrix, milestone register, risk register, data-governance notes, and export manifest.

How Fieldgrade handles evidence

Files are captured, records are fingerprinted, events are chained, review status is explicit, and exports are reproducible.

Human review by design

Fieldgrade separates capture, analysis, review, and approval. Evidence may be ingested automatically, but authority remains explicit.

Local-first by default

Sensitive evidence should not need to leave your machine just to become organised.

Accountable AI use

AI outputs are not decisions until reviewed. Fieldgrade keeps the review trail.
